How to Choose
a Medical Spa

Not all med spas are created equal. Here's what to ask before you book.

Patient Guide · 7 Min Read · By Hana Kobayashi, Patient Care Coordinator

The medical aesthetics industry is largely unregulated when it comes to who can legally perform injectables and energy-based treatments. That makes it your responsibility — and ours, as a practice — to be transparent about credentials before you ever sit in a treatment chair. Here's what to ask.

1. Who is actually performing the injection?

Ask directly: is this a physician, nurse practitioner, or registered nurse, and are they working under direct physician supervision? At Lumina, every injector operates under the direct oversight of our Medical Director, Dr. Elaine Chen.

2. What products do they use, and are they FDA-approved?

Counterfeit and unapproved filler products are a real risk in this industry. A reputable practice will show you the product packaging and confirm it's FDA-approved before every treatment.

3. How do they handle complications?

Ask what happens if you have an adverse reaction. A practice staffed by physicians should have protocols (and reversal agents, where applicable) on hand — not just a referral to urgent care.

4. Is the consultation free, and is it pressured?

Watch for practices that push you toward the most expensive package during your first visit. At Lumina, our consultation model is commission-free — we recommend only what addresses your actual goals.

5. Can you see real before-and-after results?

Ask to see a portfolio of work from the specific provider treating you, not just generic stock photography. You can view a sample of our own patient results on our Before & After page.

If a practice can't answer these questions confidently, that's information too. We're always happy to walk new patients through our credentials, products, and safety protocols before any treatment is scheduled.
